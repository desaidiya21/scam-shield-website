Cyber Safety Tips

Follow these simple cybersecurity practices to protect yourself from phishing attacks, online scams, fake websites and cybercriminals.

🔐 Never Share OTPs

Banks and trusted companies never ask for your OTP, PIN or passwords through calls, emails or messages. Keep these details private.

🌐 Check Website URLs

Always verify that the website uses HTTPS and the domain name is spelled correctly before entering personal information.

📧 Beware of Phishing Emails

Do not click on suspicious links or download unexpected attachments from unknown senders. Verify the sender before responding.

🎁 Ignore Fake Prize Messages

If you receive messages claiming that you have won a lottery or prize without entering a contest, it is most likely a scam.

🔑 Use Strong Passwords

Create long, unique passwords for every account and enable Two-Factor Authentication whenever available.

📱 Keep Software Updated

Regularly update your operating system, browser and antivirus software to stay protected against the latest security threats.

💳 Protect Financial Information

Never share your debit card number, CVV, UPI PIN or banking passwords with anyone, even if they claim to be from your bank.

🛡 Stay Alert Online

Think before you click. If a message creates urgency or asks for sensitive information, verify it through an official source before taking action.
